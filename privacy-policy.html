Privacy Policy

SMOG — Standard Medical Operating Guidelines

U.S. Army Aeromedical Evacuation · Dept. of Aviation Medicine, Fort Rucker, Alabama
Effective date: March 21, 2026 · Last updated: March 21, 2026

Short version: SMOG does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. The app works entirely offline and no data ever leaves your device.

1. Who We Are

SMOG is a medical protocol reference application developed for authorized U.S. Army aeromedical evacuation personnel, including CCFPs, ECCNs, Physician Assistants, and Flight Surgeons. This policy explains how the application handles information.

2. Information We Collect

We collect no information whatsoever. SMOG does not collect, transmit, store on external servers, or share any personal data, usage statistics, crash reports, analytics, or any other information from your device.

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The app saves the following preferences to your device's local storage only. This data never leaves your device and is inaccessible to us or any third party:

  • Your Quick Access protocol shortcuts (which protocols appear on the home screen)
  • The last protocol you viewed (for the Recent tab)
  • Your night mode display preference
  • Checklist progress for MARCH and RSI checklists (until manually reset)

This data can be cleared at any time by uninstalling the app.

4. Internet Access

SMOG operates entirely offline. The app makes no network requests of any kind. All protocol content, PDF references, calculators, checklists, and timers are bundled within the application and do not require an internet connection.

5. Third-Party Services

SMOG does not integrate any third-party analytics platforms, advertising networks, crash reporting services, or data collection SDKs.

6. Device Permissions

The app may use the following device capabilities:

  • Keep screen awake (WAKE_LOCK) — Prevents the screen from sleeping while viewing a protocol. No data is accessed or collected.
  • Screen orientation — Allows landscape viewing of protocol pages. No data is accessed or collected.

7. Intended Audience

This application is intended solely for use by authorized adult medical personnel (18 years of age or older) operating under U.S. Army aeromedical evacuation guidelines. The app is not directed at children and is not intended for general public use.

8. Medical Disclaimer

The protocols, drug information, calculators, and checklists contained in this app are provided as clinical reference aids only. They do not replace clinical judgment, current unit standing orders, or guidance from a licensed Medical Director. Always verify information against current protocols and applicable regulations.

9. Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date. Continued use of the application after any changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

10. Contact

For questions regarding this privacy policy, contact the Department of Aviation Medicine, Fort Rucker, Alabama.
